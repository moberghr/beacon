AGPL requires that:

- If you **distribute** Beacon (or a modified version), you must make the

  as a hosted/SaaS service, or expose its API/MCP endpoints to other parties),

## Which one applies to me?

| Your situation | License you need |
|---|---|
| Personal, hobby, academic, or evaluation use | AGPLv3 (free) |
| Internal use, and you're willing to publish any modifications under AGPLv3 | AGPLv3 (free) |
| You run a **modified** Beacon as a network service and **will** share your source under AGPLv3 | AGPLv3 (free) |
| You run a modified Beacon as a hosted/SaaS service but **will not** share your source | **Commercial** |
| You embed Beacon in a **closed-source / proprietary** product | **Commercial** |
| You need warranty, indemnity, or a support SLA | **Commercial** |

If you are unsure whether your use triggers the AGPL's copyleft, assume it does
and either comply or contact us for a commercial license.

## Contributing

Beacon's dual-license model only works if the project's owner holds (or is
licensed to relicense) the copyright in all contributions. Therefore **all
contributors must sign the Contributor License Agreement** before their
contributions can be merged. See [`CLA.md`](CLA.md).

This does **not** take away your rights to your own code — it grants the project
owner the rights needed to offer Beacon under both the AGPLv3 and a commercial
license.
